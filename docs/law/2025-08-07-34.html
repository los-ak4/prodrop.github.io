Prodrop has evolved quite a bit in past month. Absolute majority (100%) voted for newsual to mean “new but unsurprising and fits into one’s current worldview” and mewsual (m- morpheme + usual) to mean “something that is usual and regular but occurs relatively infrequently”. Legal proceedings are mewsual events in Prodrop culture.

Low subject unaccusatives have been proposed. Broke vase. Melted my brain. Eska’ll continue its use?

New future tense of can: cill /kɪl/ or call /kæl/. Cill + not = con’t. Other new modals: shan (< should) and wan (< would). Shan means as expected (“the monkey shan have remembered to jingyan daily”). Wan means contrary to expectations (“my ex-ex-husband wan listen to few elves one day”).

Eska + cuz = esquz. Esquz are lazy typers? No, is simply cuz are too busy using our monkey paws to gorp and need to minimize texting and screen time to increase jingyanning. Ksksay, ktf, etc.
